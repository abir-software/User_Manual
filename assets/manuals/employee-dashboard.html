Employee Dashboard — User Guide

The Employee Dashboard is your personal workspace in the System. It brings together everything you need for daily work—your profile, attendance, leaves, payslips, announcements and more—so you can view information and complete tasks quickly and confidently. Some features depend on your role (managers see extra reports and team controls). The interface is built to be simple and efficient.

Overview

Use the Dashboard as your single control center. From here you can check your status, access documents, manage attendance and request time off. Navigate by clicking the panels or links on the left and main area.

1. Personal Information (Left Side Panel)

Where to find it: left panel.

  • View your full name, job title and department.
  • See and update your profile photo and contact details (email, phone) if allowed.
  • Confirm your employment type (full-time, intern, contractor).

Action: Click My Profile to open and edit details (or request HR to update locked fields).

2. Payslips

Where to find it: Payslips panel.

  • View available payslips by month.
  • Download a PDF copy for your records.
  • Request a missing payslip using the request option (if available).

3. Contracts

Where to find it: Contracts / Documents area.

  • View your contract start/end dates and current status.
  • Download employment documents that are available to you.

4. Broad Factor (Company Leave Overview)

This panel shows colleagues who are currently on leave. Use it to coordinate work and avoid scheduling conflicts.

5. Check In / Check Out

  1. Click Check In when you start your shift.
  2. Click Check Out when you finish.

The system records your attendance automatically—verify times in Attendance History.

6. Attendance History

  • View your daily check-in/check-out records.
  • Review total working hours and flagged late entries.
  • Report discrepancies to HR if any record is incorrect.

7. Leaves

Where to find it: Leaves panel.

  • Submit new leave requests: choose leave type, dates and reason.
  • Track request status: Approved / Pending / Rejected.
  • Check your remaining leave balance before requesting time off.

8. Expenses

  • Submit expense claims and attach receipts.
  • Track approval and payment status of past claims.
  • Download copies of submitted requests for your records.

9. Upcoming Birthdays

See and celebrate colleagues’ birthdays. This small feature helps build team rapport.

10. Announcements

  • Read HR and department updates, policy changes and event notices.
  • Click an announcement to read full details and any attachments.

11. My Leave Analysis

View a visual summary of your leave activity to track trends and manage your balance.

12. My Skill Analysis

See a breakdown of skills associated with your profile (technical, soft, languages). Use it to identify development areas.

13. Leave Requests (for Employees & Managers)

Employees:

  • Submit leave requests and track their status.
  • See today’s leave count and monthly summaries.

Managers:

  • View and act on team leave requests (approve, reject, modify).
  • Manage leave allocation requests and adjust team balances.

14. Employee Graph

A pie chart shows employee distribution across departments—use it for quick workforce insights.

15. Monthly Leave Analysis

Company-wide monthly leave trends are shown here in bar and pie charts to help spot patterns by month and department.

16. Monthly Join/Resign Analysis

Track hires and resignations each month to monitor team growth and turnover.

17. Attrition Rate

The attrition metric measures employee departures over time. Managers use this to plan recruitment and retention strategies.

Conclusion

The Employee Dashboard is your all-in-one control center: check attendance, request leave, download payslips and access company updates in one place. Use it regularly to keep your information current and to manage everyday tasks efficiently. If you need help, contact HR or your manager.
